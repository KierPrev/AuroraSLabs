Lihué woke up before dawn in La Punta. The house felt strange: the microwave clock wasn’t showing the time, and the computer wouldn’t turn on. “Did the power go out?” wondered. Then, they walked to the window and pulled back the curtain.

Outside, the Sierras de San Luis looked the same as always… except for something impossible: above the hills, the sky held green curtains with touches of violet drifting slowly, as if someone were waving a theater curtain made of light.

“What is that?” whispered.

Lihué didn’t know, but the story had begun hours earlier, far from there.
